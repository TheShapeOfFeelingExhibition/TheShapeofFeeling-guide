What if feeling had a shape?
What if it were visible?

The Shape of Feeling is a fine art exhibition that explores this idea, and invites viewers on a captivating visual journey of what emotion and perception can look like, all through the creative lenses of four female Barbadian artists.

Featuring Alisha Smith ‘The Colourist’, Arianna Aleah, Jamila Greaves and King Kesia, the show reveals how feeling can transcend words and take on form to become something seen, and deeply felt. Each artist offers a personal interpretation of the theme, expressing individual experiences, narratives and insights within their work. This is emphasized using their unique visual language, compositions and colour palettes.

The adventure of The Shape of Feeling awaits and the artists are thrilled for you to explore this immersive discovery; Where colour speaks, forms evolve, and the depth of feeling takes shape.

Alisha Smith

‘The Colourist’

Alisha Smith, known as The Colourist, is a self-taught Barbadian artist and creative entrepreneur, specializing in realistic portraiture, expressive depictions of local landscapes and more recently, still lifes.

Arianna Aleah

Abstract Realism

Arianna Aleah Holligan is a 22 year-old Barbadian artist, who's works are defined by the genre of Abstract Realism. Deeply inspired by femininity and the natural world, her paintings reflect personal and cultural narratives of quiet strength.
